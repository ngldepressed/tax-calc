The OBBB Act: How the “One Big Beautiful Bill” Could Supercharge Your 2026 Tax Refund

Published: February 28, 2026

The American tax landscape has just undergone one of the most significant transformations in recent memory. With the passage of the One Big Beautiful Bill (OBBB) Act, signed into law on July 4, 2025, taxpayers are facing a 2026 filing season that could mean substantially larger refunds.

Experts project an average refund jump of $300 to $1,000. Many of these changes apply retroactively to 2025, meaning millions essentially pre-paid taxes they no longer owe.

1. The Anchors: Permanent TCJA Extensions

The OBBB Act made several 2017 provisions permanent, shielding 62% of taxpayers from a planned tax hike.

The Standard Deduction Boost

For the 2025 tax year (filed in 2026), the standard deduction thresholds are:

  • Single / Married Filing Separately: $15,750
  • Head of Household: $23,625
  • Married Filing Jointly: $31,500

2. New Deductions: Tips, Overtime, and Auto Loans

No Tax on Tips

Service workers can now deduct up to $25,000 in cash tips from taxable income. This applies to workers in 68 tipped occupations earning up to $150k (Single) or $300k (Joint).

No Tax on Overtime

The "No Tax on Overtime" provision allows a deduction of up to $12,500 for individuals and $25,000 for couples for qualified FLSA overtime.

Auto Loan Interest Deduction

Interest on auto loans for new U.S.-assembled vehicles is now deductible up to $10,000.

3. Family and Senior Support

The Child Tax Credit (CTC) rises to $2,200 per child. Additionally, seniors aged 65+ receive an extra $6,000 deduction on top of the standard amount.

4. SALT Cap Relief

In a major win for high-tax states, the SALT cap has been raised from $10,000 to $40,000 for taxpayers earning under $500,000, making itemizing viable for millions again.
